About

This was one of the first projects I ever did and as such, unfortunately, I only have the one image of it. I also do not know when I made it. Maybe sometime around 2013?
After wondering what I should model to test my skills, I decided: Why not model what is right in front of me? So after gathering a few reference images I got to work. The monitors were very simple; Just a few simple shapes glued together, not worrying about topology or real-world scale. The desk and mouse mat were a similar story. The first challenge was the keyboard - The keycaps specifically. Since I did not plan ahead, giving each key a slight curve to match my real-world keyboard (The Logitech G513) was actually surprisingly difficult. This was because some keys are longer than others. Something so simple caused me to lose so much time because I was lazy and tried to just copy-paste each key. With the slight differences in some keys, this lead to less-than-desireable results. Overall, however, it was not too bad.

The controller. My biggest challenge yet... At the time. I wish I had a close-up of this controller because it was quite tough to make. The handles especially caused a great deal of trouble thanks to their unusually unique shape.

This scene could be vastly improved with actual textures on the models and better lighting. This project taught me to plan my models before I start and that I really should work with real-world scale as much as possible. It was also a good lesson on modelling more organic looking, complex shapes. Maybe I will remake it someday - an updated desk model - and see how far I have come.
